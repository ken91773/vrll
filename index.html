What is VRLL?

Practice the hardest part of utility flying — the hover over the hook.

VRLL (Vertical Reference Longline) is a desktop simulator built for helicopter external-load work. Fly an MD 500–style ship on a longline, coordinate a swinging load, and set the hook precisely on target using real vertical-reference technique — the same picture and stick discipline used in logging, construction, powerline, and utility operations.

🪢

Real physics

Genuine pendulum dynamics: rope tension, load inertia, aerodynamic drag, and rotor downwash — no scripted shortcuts. A light hook dances; a heavy net hangs.

🎯

Fly the hook

Flight director "green dot," swing indicator, and vertical-reference camera views help you place the load on the target and hold a steady hover.

🗺

3 sq mi training area

An open rural landscape with mountains, lakes, winding roads, and working farms — realistic references for judging drift, height, and position.

🕹

Your hardware

Full joystick support with configurable axes and buttons, plus mouse-and-keyboard flying. Tunable stability augmentation and training assists.

Who it's for & why it helps

Built for pilots who want reps without burning fuel.

VRLL is supplemental rehearsal for pilots training toward — or already working in — vertical-reference external-load operations, and for anyone who wants to understand what longline flying really demands.

  • Utility & longline pilots rehearsing hover coordination and load placement between real flights.
  • Pilots in training building the vertical-reference sight picture and muscle memory.
  • Load & weight feel — swap between an empty hook, a human dummy, and a 55-gallon drum.
  • Selectable stability aids — hover hold, hover SAS, altitude hold, and auto-hover to build up gradually.
  • Guided Practice & Free Play modes so you can drill a task or just fly.
  • Repeatable & free — unlimited practice with zero operating cost.
